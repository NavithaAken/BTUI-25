About Ananda College

The Premier Buddhist School in Sri Lanka — A Proud History of Excellence in Education and Character Building

The Sacred Ananda Viharaya

Premier Buddhist School in Sri Lanka

“Appamādo Amatapadan” — *Heedfulness leads to immortality*

Located in the heart of Maradana, Colombo 10, Ananda College stands proudly as a symbol of academic excellence, discipline, and cultural pride. Founded on 1st November 1886 by Colonel Henry Steel Olcott under the Buddhist Theosophical Society, it was established to provide quality English-medium education for Buddhist children at a time when opportunities were scarce.

From its humble beginning with just thirty-seven students in Pettah, Ananda has evolved into a magnificent national school that nurtures over eight thousand students today. With its blend of tradition and modernity, the college fosters leadership, discipline, and service to the nation.

The beautiful Kularatne Hall and the modern Sathmahala Complex stand as proud symbols of Ananda’s progress through time — where generations continue to be inspired to live by noble Buddhist values and contribute meaningfully to society.

History of Mother Ananda

The Glorious Journey of Ananda College, Colombo

In the late 19th century, under the guidance of Hikkaduwe Sri Sumangala Thera, a new chapter began for Buddhist education in Sri Lanka. On a historic day, November 1, 1886, the Buddhist Theosophical Society inaugurated an English-Buddhist school at 19 Prince Street, Pettah. This humble beginning saw just 37 eager students, but it was the spark that would ignite a legacy.

As the school grew, it moved to 61 Maliban Street in 1888, accommodating 130 boys. The visionary C. W. Leadbeater became its first principal, laying the foundation for what would become Ananda College. By March 1889, the school was officially registered, boasting 120 students, with J. P. R. Weerasuriya becoming the first student to pass the prestigious Cambridge junior examination. That same year, A. E. Bultjens, a Cambridge graduate and devout Buddhist, took over as principal.

The school faced challenges, including a controversy due to its proximity to a Catholic school, prompting another move to 54 Maliban Street in 1890. However, this move only fueled its growth, with student numbers rising to 200 by 1892 and 270 by 1894. The generous donation of 3.2 acres of land by Mr. Tudor Rajapaksha led to the school's relocation to Maradana, where, on August 17, 1895, it was proudly renamed Ananda College Colombo. R. A. Mirando served as its manager until his passing during the 1915 riots.

Under the leadership of Patrick de Silva Kularatne in 1918, the school's enrollment skyrocketed from 450 to 1,000 students within two years. With a growing budget and an expanding student body, Ananda College continued to flourish. By 1961, it had officially transitioned into a government school, securing its place as a cornerstone of education in Sri Lanka.

Ananda College Old Boys’ Association (ACOBA)

A legacy of brotherhood, service, and lifelong dedication to Mother Ananda since 1908.

Origins & Formation

Founded in 1908 under the leadership of Principal Don Baron Jayatilaka, ACOBA began as an initiative to support school activities such as the annual sports meet and the traditional Old Boys’ dinner.

Early Milestones

The first recorded dinner was held on 11th August 1916 at Olcott Memorial Hall. Publications such as The Anandian (1924) and Jubilee editions carried achievements of distinguished Old Anandians.

Evolution of Leadership

Initially, the Principal served as ex-officio President. In 1960, constitutional changes allowed an Old Anandian to be elected President, while the Principal became Patron.

Branches & Expansion

The first branch—Up Country (Kandy) Branch—was founded in February 1929. Later, the ACOBA grew to include over 40 age and regional groups worldwide.

Major Contributions

ACOBA has funded major developments including the Science Block (1953), Ananda Playground (1978), Centenary Building (1986), Swimming Pool (1993), and the Olcott Statue (2001).

Publications & Legacy

ACOBA has produced many publications such as Anandaya, the 125th Anniversary Silver Coin (2011), and the Centenary Volume (1908–2008), preserving the heritage of Ananda College.

Support for Students

Beyond infrastructure, ACOBA provides sponsorships and financial assistance to needy students, ensuring educational empowerment across generations.
