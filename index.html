About Ananda College

The Premier Buddhist School in Sri Lanka — A Proud History of Excellence in Education and Character Building

The Sacred Ananda Viharaya

Premier Buddhist School in Sri Lanka

“Appamādo Amatapadan” — *Heedfulness leads to immortality*

Located in the heart of Maradana, Colombo 10, Ananda College stands proudly as a symbol of academic excellence, discipline, and cultural pride. Founded on 1st November 1886 by Colonel Henry Steel Olcott under the Buddhist Theosophical Society, it was established to provide quality English-medium education for Buddhist children at a time when opportunities were scarce.

From its humble beginning with just thirty-seven students in Pettah, Ananda has evolved into a magnificent national school that nurtures over eight thousand students today. With its blend of tradition and modernity, the college fosters leadership, discipline, and service to the nation.

The beautiful Kularatne Hall and the modern Sathmahala Complex stand as proud symbols of Ananda’s progress through time — where generations continue to be inspired to live by noble Buddhist values and contribute meaningfully to society.

History of Mother Ananda

The Glorious Journey of Ananda College, Colombo

Ananda College, one of Sri Lanka’s most prestigious educational institutions, was founded on 1st November 1886 by the Buddhist Theosophical Society under the vision of Colonel Henry Steel Olcott. The college began with just 37 students in a humble house in Pettah, serving as a beacon of Buddhist education during a time when Buddhist children had limited opportunities in English-medium schools.

With the guidance of devoted educators and philanthropists such as Mr. C. W. Leadbeater and Don Baron Jayatilaka, Ananda College rapidly grew in reputation and student numbers. In 1895, the school was relocated to its current site at Maradana, Colombo 10, marking a new era in its development.

Over the decades, Ananda College became not only a center of academic excellence but also a stronghold of patriotism and national identity. Many distinguished leaders, scholars, and heroes of Sri Lanka have walked through its gates — upholding the noble motto, “Appamādo Amatapadam” — meaning *Heedfulness is the path to the Deathless*.

Today, Ananda College continues to inspire generations with its unwavering commitment to discipline, knowledge, and Buddhist values, remaining the premier Buddhist school in the nation.
